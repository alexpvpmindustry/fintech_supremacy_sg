Q: How is the net gain per trade calculated?
A: The net gain per trade is calculated by subtracting the fee rate from the return rate. In this calculator, with a 2.8% return and 0.3% fee, your net gain is 2.5% per trade. This means for every $100 in your account, you earn $2.80 but pay $0.30 in fees, netting $2.50 profit.
Q: Is a 2.8% return per trade realistic?
A: While 2.8% per trade is achievable for experienced traders in favorable market conditions, it's important to note that consistent returns are difficult to maintain. This calculator serves as a planning tool to visualize potential growth, but actual trading results will vary based on market conditions, strategy, and risk management. Always trade responsibly and never risk more than you can afford to lose.
Q: Why does it take 170 trades to reach $1 million from $15,000?
A: This is the power of compound interest at work. With a consistent 2.5% net gain per trade, your principal grows exponentially rather than linearly. Each trade's profit becomes part of your new principal, which then generates even more profit on the next trade. This compounding effect accelerates your growth over time, which is why the path from $15,000 to $1 million requires 170 successful trades.
Q: What happens if I have losing trades?
A: This calculator assumes consistent positive returns on every trade. In real trading, losses are inevitable. A single losing trade can set back your progress significantly because it reduces your principal. For example, a 2.5% loss requires a 2.56% gain just to break even. This is why risk management, stop losses, and proper position sizing are crucial for long-term trading success.
